Edward Hyeonbeen Lee

I am an incoming PhD student at Virginia Tech, where I'll work with Prof. Simon Stepputtis in the TEA Lab. My research interests include physics-aware machine learning and neurosymbolic AI for contact-rich robotics.

Before joining Virginia Tech, I received my B.Eng. and M.Eng. degrees in Mechanical Engineering from Kyung Hee University, where I was advised by Prof. Jin-Gyun Kim in the Modeling & Simulation Lab. During my master's studies, I was also mentored by Prof. Hee-Sun Choi at Sejong University and Prof. Seongji Han at Chungnam National University. In 2023, I was fortunate to work with Prof. Joseph Lim in the CLVR Lab at KAIST AI.

I usually go by Edward, but you can also call me Hyeonbeen, which is my given name in South Korea.

Research

I'm currently interested in developing physics-aware neurosymbolic policies for efficient, interpretable, and generalizable control of robot-environment interactions. At Kyung Hee University, I worked on data-driven modeling of nonlinear dynamics in contact- and vibration-rich physical systems.
